HMONG VILLAGE SHOPPING CENTER: ST. PAUL'S UNIQUE CULINARY DESTINATION

Overview
Hmong Village is a one-of-a-kind indoor market in the United States, located in the heart of Eastside St. Paul. This sprawling shopping center combines dining, grocery shopping, cultural goods, and professional services all under one roof, making it a central hub for Hmong and Southeast Asian communities.

Location
Heart of Eastside St. Paul, Minnesota
Convenient access for Metro State University students

What Sets Hmong Village Apart
Unlike typical shopping malls, Hmong Village features over 250 vendors representing authentic Hmong and Southeast Asian businesses. It's designed as an immersive cultural experience where you can taste authentic foods, shop for specialty groceries, and experience unique goods and services.

Food & Dining Options
Food Court: Multiple dining options with 17 restaurants and/or delis
Fresh Groceries: On-site market with specialty ingredients
Authentic Southeast Asian Cuisine: Direct from food vendors with roots in Hmong and Southeast Asian culinary traditions

Restaurant & Deli Highlights
The food court features 17 different restaurants and delis, each offering authentic Hmong and Southeast Asian cuisine. Options include:
- Traditional pho and noodle dishes
- Regional specialties from Hmong cuisine
- Authentic Southeast Asian preparations
- Fresh ingredients and family recipes

Why Food Lovers Visit
Fresh, authentic flavors prepared by communities with deep culinary roots. Every dish reflects traditional preparation methods and genuine family recipes. The food court atmosphere lets you experience multiple cuisines in one visit.

Visitor Experience
Walking through Hmong Village, you'll encounter a vibrant marketplace atmosphere. The 250+ vendors create an authentic shopping and dining environment. Many customers are regulars who return for specific vendors and trusted food sources.

Cultural Significance
Hmong Village represents a major economic and cultural hub for the Twin Cities' Hmong community. It's a place where cultural traditions, modern business, and community connection thrive together.
